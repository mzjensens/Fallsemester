Class Information

The Materials and Processes course offers a comprehensive exploration of the materials, manufacturing processes, and design techniques essential for creating products aimed at mass and rapid production. The curriculum emphasizes understanding the impact of diverse materials and processes on the industrial design workflow. Students will examine the mechanical and physical properties of various materials and their implications for manufacturing processes, equipment, environmental concerns, and end-user requirements. The course aims to provide practical insights into material selection, application strategies, and process specification, focusing on practical rather than theoretical aspects of materials science. This hands-on approach ensures that students are well-prepared to make informed decisions in real-world industrial design projects.

Professor Information

Jonas Hauptman is a Professor of Industrial Design at Virginia Tech and a co-leader of the BioDesign Research Group. His innovative work focuses on integrating digital technologies with biological and bio-inspired materials to design products and buildings that respond intelligently to human needs while having a positive ecological impact. A significant part of his research involves sustainable building methods using bamboo, aiming to create eco-friendly and efficient building solutions. Hauptman has founded several award-winning enterprises that leverage specialized fabrication techniques with novel material approaches. His academic and professional journey includes significant contributions to the field of design, evidenced by his involvement in prestigious projects and receipt of multiple awards such as the Autodesk ACADIA Emerging Research Award and the AIA Upjohn award. His teaching includes courses on material and process of industrial design and advanced design laboratory, where he integrates his research into student learning, emphasizing sustainability and innovative material use in design.
