Class information

Creating Intelligent Content is a technical communication course that equips students with the skills to develop adaptable and reusable content across various platforms. The curriculum focuses on structured authoring, the use of metadata, content management systems, and the integration of artificial intelligence to enhance content efficiency and delivery. Through lectures, case studies, and hands-on projects, students will learn to effectively manage and streamline content, preparing them for careers in content strategy and digital communication.

Professor information

Professor: Carlos Evia is a distinguished member of the faculty at Virginia Tech, serving as the Associate Dean for Transdisciplinary Initiatives and Chief Technology Officer in the College of Liberal Arts and Human Sciences. He's also a Professor of Communication and the Director of the Academy of Transdisciplinary Studies at Virginia Tech. His primary research focuses on developing technology-based solutions for workplace communication challenges, particularly those involving multicultural audiences. This often involves structured authoring and content strategy, where he utilizes the Darwin Information Typing Architecture (DITA) and Lightweight DITA to structure, reuse, and publish content efficiently across various platforms. His work emphasizes making digital communication efficient, scalable, and cost-effective. Professor Evia has made significant contributions through his publications, including books like "Creating Intelligent Content with Lightweight DITA," and he's actively involved in professional roles such as the Lightweight DITA Subcommittee and the Darwin Information Typing Architecture Technical Committee.
