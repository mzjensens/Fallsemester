Class Information

The Topics in Design Competencies course provides an immersive introduction to digital fabrication, focusing on CNC (Computer Numerical Control) machining and laser cutting techniques. Students explore the entire digital fabrication process, from design to execution, learning to integrate tools like Fusion 360 for CAM (Computer-Aided Manufacturing). The curriculum emphasizes the strategic selection and application of digital tools, considering their specific advantages and limitations. Through practical, hands-on projects, students develop proficiency in operating CNC machines and begin to understand laser cutting, enhancing their ability to infuse creativity into their digital designs. This course equips students with foundational skills in modern manufacturing processes, preparing them for advanced applications in design and production.

Professor Information

This class is being taught primarily by Yasaman Ashjazadeh with Jonas Hauptman supervising. Yasaman Ashjazadeh is currently pursuing her Master of Architecture at Virginia Tech and is involved with the BioDesign Research Group. Her academic focus aligns with the use of sustainable materials and innovative design practices in architecture. Yasaman is part of a team that explores the intersections of biology, design, and sustainability, contributing to projects that may reshape how building materials are understood and utilized in the architectural field.
